Sheets update

February 5, 2009

According to Brewers.com, the official website of the Milwaukee Brewers, the reason Ben Sheets has not been signed yet is because of a lingering elbow issue that may require surgery. The issue is whether or not the Brewers will have to pay for the surgery. It is uncertain at this time if Sheets will be ready to go for opening day, but he has yet to sign with a major league team. He had a contract pending with the Texas Rangers, but did not pass the physical, leaving his 2009 status uncertain. Read the full story at Brewers.com

2008-09 off-season assessment

February 5, 2009

Let’s take a look at the Brewers’ offseason moves to see what kind of shape they are in heading into the 2009 campaign. It’s tempting to call C.C. Sabathia the biggest loss, but we have to remember that C.C. only played in 17 games for the Crew. The Brewers set themselves up for a 2007 sprint to the finish without Sabathia, and, while it is likely that without him they would not have made the playoffs in 2008, they still would have been in decent shape to make a run at the end. I think a bigger loss is that of closer Salomon Torres, who retired after last season. Luckily, the Brewers plugged that hole with all-time saves leader Trevor Hoffman. True, Hoffman is getting up there in years, but this fan thinks that at age 41 he still has enough left in the tank to seal a substantial amount of victories, assuming the starting pitching can put enough games in his hands.

The entire starting line-up is returning this year, which to me is the most exciting part. They certainly scored enough runs to win ball games, and it seems that Corey Hart, Ryan Braun, and Prince Fielder seem to be getting better every year. The only question mark I have is the defensive skills of Rickie Weeks. I think his inability to get the ball out of the glove cost the Brewers a few runs and a couple of games last year, and his offense certainly wasn’t anything to talk about, but he has shown potential at the plate and I think the prospect of returning the entire starting lineup is worth leaving him in there, at least to start the season.

The starting rotation is a question mark, but that seems to be a yearly ritual in Milwaukee. Nobody is going to go out there and shred opposing line-ups, but it seems that no matter who they throw out there they pitch just good enough to win just enough ball games to keep the season exciting until the end. The rotation looks to be, Yovani Gallardo, Dave Bush, Jeff Suppan, Manny Parra, and Seth McClung, with Chris Capuano being a long shot to take a spot in May. Capuano is coming off his second Tommy John surgery and has yet to test his arm. If he can come back to close to the way he pitched in 2005 and 2006, he will be a real boost to the team. Ben Sheets is still a wild-card, too. The free agent has been looking for a lucrative deal and is most likely headed to Texas to reunite with former Brewer pitching coach Mike Maddux. However, one would have to think that the longer he waits, the lower his price will get and Doug Melvin has not entirely ruled out the possibility of bringing him back. Chase Wright, recently acquired in a trade with the Yankees, could be a possibility also, but looks to be more of a middle reliever if he makes the team at all.

The bullpen is the shakiest are in my opinion. Hoffman, who was 30 of 34 in save opportunities with San Diego last season, can get the job done if given the opportunity. Without a true set-up man, though, it remains to be seen if the Brewers bullpen troubles will continue this season. Dave Riske and Carlos Villanueva have shown they can get the job done on occasion, but the rest of the ‘pen will really have to step it up this year given the inexperience of the starters.

Last but not least, Ken Macha has been handed the keys to the team and is hoping to be the catalyst that Ned Yost had failed to be the last two seasons to make everything come together. Macha comes to Milwaukee by way of the Oakland Athletics and put up some pretty good numbers there. In 4 seasons he won the division twice and finished second twice. Hopefully he can bring some of that magic with him.

All in all, 2009 looks to be a very exciting season. All of this baseball talk is getting me antsy. I can’t wait to hear Uecker again, and I can smell the brats cooking in the Miller Park parking lot already. I hope to see everyone out supporting our Brew Crew again this year.

2008 Season in Review

February 4, 2009

I think my first order of business will be to bask in the accomplishments of last year. The Brew Crew finally broke their 26 year playoff drought with a wild card appearance against the Philadelphia Phillies. It wasn’t easy by any means, but they managed to hold everybody off with the help of the Superhuman arm of C.C. Sabathia. I really thought if the Brewers could have pulled off a Sunday victory at Miller Park that C.C.’s arm would have propelled them past Philly in game 5 and on to their first ever N.L.C.S. appearance.

Along the way was a difficult decision by Doug Melvin and Mark Attanasio to relieve skipper Ned Yost of his duties. I had never been a big Yost fan, and was among those calling for his head following the 2007 season-ending debacle, but I didn’t think Yost’s moves warranted his firing. In the end, it proved to be the right decision, but certainly not because of the magic or genius provided by Dale Sveum. Maybe Yost had put in his 8 innings for the year and they just needed to bring in a closer. The bottom line is, it worked.

Solid performances by Ryan Braun, Prince Fielder, J.J. Hardy, and Corey Hart helped prop the Brewers up when the pitching let them down. The pitching did not blow anybody away, at least not until the addition of Sabathia at mid-season. Sabathia managed to have the second highest win total on the team despite only pitching in 17 games. His 11 wins were topped only by Ben Sheets with his career high 13 wins. Surely everyone on the pitching staff would have had more wins if the closing situation had cleared itself up earlier. Just 10 out of 17 in save attempts, Eric Gagne was the frequent target of fans early in the year. He finished the season with a lofty 5.44 ERA. Salomon Torres stepped into the closing role and shored things up a bit for the Crew.

All in all it was a pretty good year for our Boys in Blue. Hopefully, this coming season will bring more of the same, although it is difficult at first glance to see how the Brewers will fill the gaping hole caused by the departure of Sabathia. More on the coming season in future posts, though.
